How I sell

Sales philosophy

After a career of carrying quota across enterprise services and SaaS, I've learned that the sellers who consistently win aren't the most aggressive — they're the ones who understand the customer's outcome better than anyone else in the room. When I can draw a clear line between what the customer is trying to achieve and the value I'm delivering, price becomes a secondary conversation.
01
Lead with the outcome, not the product
Every engagement starts with understanding what success looks like for the customer — not what I'm trying to close. I connect their desired business outcomes to tangible, measurable value before features or pricing ever enter the conversation. That's how you earn the right to propose a bolder solution — and close at a higher ACV than a price-led seller ever could.
"I could sell at $5 and deliver zero value. Or I could fully understand their needs, build champions, and sell at $15 — because the value is measured in business outcomes, not dollars."
Outcome-first selling
02
Teach something they don't already know
Challenger-informed in how I engage buyers: I lead with insight, reframe how customers think about their future state, and tailor messaging to specific personas and priorities. The best discovery question isn't about current state — it's the one that reframes the future and positions you as the person who helped them see it. Sellers who challenge thinking earn the credibility to propose bolder solutions.
Insight-led selling creates the kind of trust that turns a vendor relationship into a strategic partnership — and a strategic partnership into multi-year, expanded contracts.
Challenger · Insight-led
03
Build multi-threaded conviction
Individual champions don't close deals — buying committees do. I map stakeholders across the organization, build relationships at multiple levels, and create internal champions who carry the value narrative when I'm not in the room. MEDDPICC and Miller Heiman LAMP applied where no structured methodology previously existed, creating frameworks that elevated the entire team's execution.
When you bring multiple champions along for the journey, you don't have to fight on price. You've earned the right to sell a more valuable solution — because you're delivering more value.
MEDDPICC · LAMP · Multi-thread
Innovation in practice

AI as a competitive advantage

The approach
Building what the tech stack couldn't deliver.
Most sellers use AI to write emails faster. I use it to build automations that solve systemic gaps — creating intelligence layers and workflows that Salesforce, Gong, and standard outbound tools simply don't provide natively.

Across Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT, I've built a personal operating system for enterprise selling: automated prospect scoring, pre-meeting intelligence briefings, CRM overlays, and GTM research tools that run continuously across a 300+ account book — without a dedicated ops team or custom software budget.

The goal was never to save time. It was to create capabilities that didn't exist before — and use them to drive outcomes that simply wouldn't be achievable at this scale without them. AI isn't a feature of how I work. It's the force multiplier behind everything I do.
